Relational Database

  1. Entity: A real-world object or concept represented in a database as a table.
  2. Attribute: in ER diagram is a property or characteristic of an entity represented by a named column in the corresponding database table.
  3. Relationship: The association between two or more entities.
  4. Generalization/Specialization: A type of relationship in which a parent entity (generalization) is related to multiple child entities (specialization) that inherit its attributes and constraints.
  5. Weak Entities: An entity that cannot be identified by its own attributes and must be related to another entity (identifying entity) to be uniquely identified.
  6. Aggregation: A type of relationship in which one entity is a part of another entity.
  7. Participation Constraints: Integrity constraints that specify whether an entity must participate in a relationship with another entity.
  8. Key Constraints: Integrity constraints that enforce the uniqueness of the values in a specific set of columns (composite key) and ensure that no column can have null values.

Participation constraints: a rule that defines the participation of entities in a relationship. It indicates if all entities participate in the relationship. An entity’s participation can be total or partial.

  1. Total participation: means that all entities of an entity set must participate in the relationship, it is shown by a thick line.
  2. Partial participation: means that not all entities of an entity set need to participate in the relationship, it is shown by a thin line.
  • Total participation is important in deletions as it ensures that all related entities are deleted when the main entity is deleted.

Covering constraints:

  1. Total specialization: a superclass entity (a more general entity) must belong to at least one subclass (a more specific entity) and cannot exist without being associated with a subclass. This is also known as a total specialization.
  • e.g. A superclass entity of “Animals” must belong to at least one subclass such as “Mammals”, “Birds” or “Fish” and cannot exist without being associated with a subclass.
  1. Partial specialization: a superclass entity may not be associated with any subclass. This means that some superclass entities may not have any corresponding subclass entities. This is also known as a partial specialization.
  • e.g. A superclass entity of “Employees” may not be associated with any subclass such as “Full-time” or “Part-time”, some employees may not have any corresponding subclass entities.
  1. Weak entity: an entity that does not have a unique identifier and can only be identified in the context of a related, stronger entity called the owner entity.
  2. Attributes: characteristics or properties of an entity.
  3. Entity: a real-world object or concept that can be represented by a set of attributes.
    For example, an “Employee” entity can have attributes such as “name”, “age”, “address” etc. , while the “address” attribute has its own characteristics like street, city, zip code.
    • The main difference between attributes and entities is that attributes are properties of an entity, whereas entities are real-world objects or concepts that can be represented by a set of attributes. Additionally, attributes cannot have multiple values and entities can be connected to other entities through relationships.
